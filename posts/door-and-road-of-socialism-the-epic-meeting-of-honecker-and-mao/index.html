Door and Road of Socialism - The Epic Meeting of Honecker and Mao

June 11, 2017

course thesis: introduction to Mao Zedong thoughts and theoretical system of socialism with Chinese characteristic

This fiction does not represent the author political position.

To my mind, the so-called “socialist society” is not anything immutable. Like all other social formations, it should be conceived in a state of constant flux and change. ——Friedrich Engels

On October 29, 1976, the General Secretary of the Socialist Unity Party of Germany, Erich Honecker, was elected chairman of the Council of State of German Democratic Republic. Flocked with flowers, among applause and cheers, Honecker smiled and walked into his spacious new office.

Squeak-bang.

With a muffled sound, the heavy door was gently closed. In an instant, those excitement seemed to be isolated in another world. Honecker’s all-day-long stressed body relaxed, his brows froze and he sighed deeply.

He was too tired.

The baton of the Republic, a burning hot baton, was passed into his hands. This country, born in the Soviet-occupied region after World War II, was considered by most people to be a satellite state of the Soviet Union; a large part of the population did not agree with the country’s political and economic system. Despite the construction of the Berlin Wall, there were still East German residents illegally fleeing to West Germany; the national economy was unable to meet the residents ’demand for certain food and high-end consumer goods, which had to be imported from the West. The country ’s debt was like a rolling snow ball, growing bigger and bigger. Honecker had always believed in Communism faithfully, but why was the road of communism getting more and more bumpy, without an end? It suddenly occurred to him that more than 7,000 kilometers away, there was a socialist country, the People’s Republic of China, which was born only six days before the German Democratic Republic. It had just experienced a decade of cultural catastrophe. At this moment it was full of scars, and its people suffering. In comparison, the pain of Democratic Germany seemed to be less unbearable. So he straightened his body again, cheered up his spirits, went to the desk and sat down, began thinking seriously about the future of the country.

“Reform is imperative,” Honecker said contemplatively to himself, “Restrictions in the field of culture and art must be lifted. Freedom and harmony are what people want… We have reached an agreement with the Federal Republic of Germany. After the The Basic Treaty was concluded, we should also visit West Germany to promote communication and reconciliation, and follow the general trend of peace in the world … Oh! West Germany! Abhorrent West Germany! I am so angry when I think of it! What ’s so attractive about the decaying and sinful Capitalism! Why do our people plunge into the embrace of Capitalism! Alas! It seems that we must strengthen the Ministry of National Security, not only to vigorously suppress domestic political dissidents, but also to monitor the people in order to consolidate the power of our Socialist Unity Party. And the economy, oh … how about promoting centralized economy? Well, it sounds like a wise idea … “

“This is not necessarily the case.” came a deep, rich voice.

Who is speaking? Honecker was startled, and turned around to look. Sitting opposite the desk was an unknown old man with an oriental face. Who is he? How did he come in? Honecker jumped up from the desk, trying to call for the guard. But somehow he couldn’t make any sound. The old man in front of him sat calmly and watched him quietly, with his dignified and gentle eyes.

Suddenly Honecker gulped: “You… you are Mao!?”

The old man acquiesced without a nod or a shake of head.

“But you DIED last month! Are you a ghost?” Honecker broke out in a cold sweat. Having had ups and downs floating in the political sea for more than 40 years, he had seen more than enough. However, it was his first time in life seeing a dead person talking.

Honecker tried to stay calm and sat down slowly. There was disturbing silence in the air.

“You … what brings you to Germany?” Unnak couldn’t help but break the silence first.

Mao cast his eyes out of the window, looking out at the East, and spoke with melancholy: “Comrade Honecker, you are right. I am a deceased man. But I still care about the struggles of the world, the development of Communism, so for more than a month, my soul is still wandering among the Socialist camps. The field trips these days have given me a deeper understanding of Socialism than when I was alive. I came here to see for myself, whether the policies implemented by one of our most developed Socialist ally, East Germany, are of referential or warning values for China. Unfortunately, my time has passed, “speaking of this, he looked at Honecker again. “The past is in the past! For truly great men, look to this age alone.”

Honecker was intrigued by Mao’s words. He asked curiously: “What profound understanding do you have? Just now you said that the economic reform measures I was thinking about may not be a good idea. Why?”

Mao smiled and said, “Don’t worry. Would you mind talking to me first about the current situation and your thoughts?”

Honecker cleared his throat and confidently talked: “It’s like this. We are implementing a “trust” large-scale enterprise system with centralized leadership and unified planning. In early 1964, we began to implement the guiding ideology in a comprehensive way, which was widely utilizing economic laws and material benefit principles, implementing a new economic system that combines planning and markets, centralization and decentralization. The main contents of the reform were: First, expanding the autonomy of enterprises and localities. Second, highlighting the use of profit-centric economic leverage; reducing mandatory planning indicators; the state no longer intervenes too much in the production, sales, and finance of enterprises; and enterprises only accept a small number of value indicators centered on profits. Third, enterprises enjoy greater investment decision-making power; funds are mainly self-raised; and insufficient loans are made in banks. Fourth, the banking system should be reformed to separate the functions of the national bank from commercial banks, replace the former national investment bank with a new industrial and commercial bank, and implement a profit-oriented “active loan policy”. Fifth, the prices of industrial products have been reformed, and so on. “

“Very detailed system reform,” Mao applauded, “what next? What did this new economic system bring to the country? Is it still being implemented?

Honecker continued: “I have to admit that the new economic system has achieved some success in breaking excessive concentration and activating economy. The rate of economic development has increased compared with before the reform. However, since 1969 and 1970, the rate of development has slowed down. Some major indicators of the Five-Year Plan have not been completed. Major problems have emerged such as the fragmentation of funds, the dispersion of materials, and the decline in the quality of products. The Central Party has analyzed the economic situation and believes that the new economic system has become too fragmented and that economic development exists a trend of “self-flow”. Therefore, we must put a brake! We must resolutely stop implementing the plan of the new economic system approved by the government, gradually restore and strengthen the centralized leadership system … Comrade Mao, what are you writing? “

Mao just took out a small red book from his pocket and wrote down on the inside of the cover: “In a socialist economy, under the dominancy of public ownership of the means of production, it is necessary to strengthen centralized leadership and planning management, “he replied,“I am taking notes! Please keep on. “

“Okay. Therefore, we have gradually adjusted the contraction since the early 1970s. Especially the investment management of enterprises and the allocation and management of important materials are very strict. But we didn’t simply restored the system of the 50s, rather, we retained some of the effective economic solutions of the 1960s. “

“Oh? How effective was that? I appreciate further details.”

“To be specific, first, we adhere to and improve a centralized and unified plan management system. We emphasize that plan is the core of economic management, and advocate that all economic activities should be balanced and regulated by the state plan, and support neither market regulation nor guiding plan. Second, through the establishment of large-scale trust-type joint ventures, we realize the combination and enlargement of enterprises, simplifying management hierarchy. Third, under the guidance of the state plan, we emphasize the state’s manipulation and use of economic leverage to promote the realization of the planned goals. The fourth is price reform. These contractionary adjustment reforms have been very effective, and I think we can step up further efforts.”

Mao contemplated, muttered to himself: “When socialist countries begin to explore the path of Socialism, they all hope to continuously improve the socialist system through reform. Reform is not only an essential requirement for the self-improvement and continuous development of Socialism. It is also the objective law of the development of a socialist society … “

“In short, in terms of economic construction, our party has long followed the Stalin model and firmly believes that the planned economy is the essence of Socialism. The characteristics of our planned economy system are from top to down, and everything is produced according to plan.

“Comrade Honecker, I don’t know if I should say something inappropriately.” Mao said.

“Feel free to give it.”

“In my opinion, the reason why productivity began to decline again is because of the incomplete reform. In the face of severe economic situation, your party is not solving the problem by continuing to deepen the reform and liberating the productive forces to the maximum extent, but is addicted to the so-called “perfection” of repairing the established plan, and finally returns to the old road of planned economy. The socialist road of your country is deeply influenced by the Soviet model. There are serious defects in the system, and inevitably many difficulties in practice. How to further improve the productivity, so as to improve the material and cultural living standards of the people of East Germany, narrow the gap with West Germany, stabilize people’s minds, and prevent the loss of personnel is a major issue in front of your party. “

“Is it wrong to draw on the experience of the Soviet Union?”

Mao looked up and said, “Twenty years ago, Ulbricht led a delegation to Beijing to participate in the 8th National Congress of China Communist Party. We held a two-hour private talk. It is as vivid as if it were yesterday! We shared with each other that time our own experiences, but our conditions were different, and our experience was for reference only. We used to draw all the experience from the Soviet Union in the past, but later it proved to be inconsistent with our specific conditions. The lack of independent exploration will eventually lead to failure of Socialism!”

“You mean that something went wrong with our reforms?” Honecker’s fair eyebrows were raised and he said in confusion.

“Historical materialism tells us that contradictions are both universal and specific. This month after my death, I have visited many countries and heard the reform experiences of leaders in various countries. There are successes and failures. These experiences really benefited me a lot. However, if the socialist road wants to be successfully explored and healthily developed, the key is to combine the basic principles of Marxism with the specific realities of the country under new historical conditions. We should always be realistic, take our own path and build Socialism with our own characteristics. It is absolutely difficult to succeed if we copy the experience of other countries. “

Honecker froze for a second, and then sneered, “Oh, how incisive you are. Look at you, in the last ten years of your life, what did the Cultural Revolution you launched bring to China? I am confident to lead the Republic towards a better future! Excuse me for not seeing you out.”

For a while, Mao was lost in thoughts. After a moment, he came to himself and laughed out aloud, “Hahahaha! Go and ask the heaven and the earth, who is calling the shot? All are gone, all are gone…” He breezed out in laughter.

Squeak–

„Erich, es ist Zeit zum Abendessen. Erich? Bist du eingeschlafen?“ (“Erich, it is dinner time. Erich? Are you asleep?”)

„Was… Margot? Wo ist Mao? War das nur ein Traum?“(“What … Margot? Where is Mao? Was that just a dream?”)

Humping over the desk, Honecker raised his heavy eyelid and looked at the door. The golden chandelier outside the door seemed particularly dazzling to Honecker, who had just woke up, and the light flowed wildly into the dim office. Honecker had to squint his eyes. In a trance, he saw the door of Communism slowly opening in front of him, and behind it was a golden future …

Postscript

Although East Germany has carried out economic reforms many times, this kind of reform has not been able to persist in the end. The fundamental reason is that the economic system reform has not stepped out of the “Soviet model”. This highly centralized economic system that relies on administrative means, exclusion of market regulation, and purely planned management, does not meet the national conditions of East Germany, nor can it meet the urgent requirements of economic intensification, which has largely restrained the development of productivity, become the resistance to socialist construction in East Germany. In the end, the domestic economic crisis broke out frequently, the people lived a hard life, lost confidence in socialism, and fled. The people actually spurned the Socialist Unity Party.

This negative example fully illustrates that we should break through the ideological framework that the planned economy is a basic characteristic of a socialist society, and find a way to build socialism that suits our national conditions. China and East Germany experienced similar shift from a highly centralized planned economy to a market economy. At the end of this novel, Honecker was confident that he could march towards a “golden future”, but in fact he could not avoid the fate of failure to explore the socialist road. East Germany did not finally knock on the door of Communism and retreated; while China is still firmly on the road. Can we finally embrace “the bright future”? Let us wait and see.
